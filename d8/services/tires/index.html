Signs You Need Tire Service

Some tire issues are obvious the moment you see them; others build up gradually. Here's what's worth a look:

  • Low or uneven tread wear across the tire.
  • A tire that seems to lose air faster than the others.
  • A vibration or thump that shows up at speed.
  • Visible sidewall damage, bulging, or a nail or screw stuck in the tread.
  • A TPMS or tire pressure warning light on the dash.
  • The car pulling to one side, which can point to uneven tire wear or pressure.

A slow leak or a worn tire rarely fixes itself, and catching it early is usually the difference between a patch and a replacement.

Why Tire Service Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Cold air makes tire pressure drop noticeably — a tire can lose several PSI just from a hard overnight freeze, which is exactly when good tread and correct pressure matter most for traction on snow and ice. The potholes that show up every spring after a freeze-thaw cycle can also damage a tire's sidewall or throw off alignment in ways that wear tires unevenly. Road salt doesn't affect the rubber itself much, but it does speed up corrosion on wheels and valve stems over a season. Checking your tires before winter driving conditions arrive, not after a close call on ice, is time well spent.
