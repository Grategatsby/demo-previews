Signs You're Due for an Oil Change or Maintenance Visit

Some maintenance needs are easy to track; others sneak up between visits. Here's what's worth paying attention to:

  • Your oil-change or maintenance light comes on.
  • It's been a while since your last oil change and you're not sure how long.
  • Oil on the dipstick looks dark, gritty, or low.
  • A new engine noise, tick, or rattle that wasn't there before.
  • A belt squeal on startup or when accessories like the AC kick on.
  • You're planning a long road trip and want peace of mind before you go.

None of these need to wait for a scheduled visit — if something's nagging at you, it's worth a call.

Why Routine Maintenance Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Cold starts are hard on an engine in ways that build up slowly over a season. Oil thickens in freezing temperatures, which means an engine works harder in the first few minutes after a cold start — exactly when fresh oil and a healthy battery matter most. Road salt used to clear South St Paul streets can also work its way into components under the hood over a winter, and a belt or hose that's already showing wear is more likely to fail in extreme cold than in a mild summer. A routine oil change and maintenance check before winter sets in, or partway through it, catches small issues while they're still small.
