What Our Tune-Up & Oil Change Service Covers

Routine maintenance covers more ground than just the oil, and we check the full picture while your vehicle is in:

  • Oil and filter changes — using the oil weight and type your vehicle calls for.
  • Multi-point inspection — a look at belts, hoses, fluids, and other wear items while the vehicle is already up.
  • Spark plug and ignition checks — addressing rough idling, misfires, or reduced fuel economy.
  • Filter replacement — air and cabin filters that affect performance and air quality inside the vehicle.
  • Fluid level checks — coolant, brake fluid, washer fluid, and other levels topped off as needed.
  • Battery and charging system checks — especially important heading into a Minnesota winter.

If it's a routine item that keeps your vehicle running reliably, it's part of what we check.

Signs Your Vehicle Is Due for Service

Some maintenance needs are marked on a sticker, but plenty show up in how the vehicle feels. Here's what's worth a call:

  • An oil life or maintenance reminder light on the dash.
  • It's been longer than the recommended interval since your last oil change.
  • A rough idle, hesitation, or a noticeable drop in fuel economy.
  • The engine feels less responsive than it used to.
  • Oil that looks dark and gritty on the dipstick.
  • You're not sure when the last tune up or oil change actually happened.

None of these need to turn into a bigger repair. Staying ahead of routine maintenance is the simplest, least expensive way to keep a vehicle reliable.

Why Routine Maintenance Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Cold starts are hard on an engine, and oil that's overdue for a change doesn't protect moving parts the way fresh oil does, especially on the coldest mornings of the year. A weak battery that was borderline in October often fails outright once temperatures really drop, which is exactly why a tune up before winter includes a look at the battery and charging system. Road salt and the stop-and-go driving that comes with winter conditions put extra strain on belts, hoses, and filters over a season. Getting an oil change and tune up done before winter driving conditions arrive is one of the simplest ways to avoid getting stranded on a cold morning.
