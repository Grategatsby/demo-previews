What Our Auto Repair Service Covers

General auto repair is the broadest part of what we do, and it covers a lot of ground:

  • Check-engine and warning light diagnosis — figuring out what triggered the light, not just clearing the code.
  • Engine performance issues — rough idling, stalling, hesitation, or a loss of power.
  • Starting and charging system problems — batteries, alternators, and starters, especially in a Minnesota winter.
  • Unusual noises — rattles, clunks, squeals, and grinding that shouldn't be there.
  • Fluid leaks — oil, coolant, transmission fluid, or anything pooling under your car.
  • Suspension and steering — a car that pulls, wanders, or rides rougher than it used to.
  • Exhaust system issues — new noises from underneath, or a failed emissions check.

If it's under the hood or underneath the car, we can take a look and give you a straight answer about what's going on — on foreign and domestic vehicles alike.

Signs Your Car Needs a Repair Visit

Some problems are obvious. Others are easy to put off until they get expensive. Here are common signs it's time to bring your vehicle in:

  • A check-engine, battery, or oil pressure light stays on (or flashes).
  • The car is harder to start than it used to be, especially on cold mornings.
  • You hear a new squeal, grind, clunk, or rattle that wasn't there before.
  • The car pulls to one side, vibrates at speed, or the steering feels loose.
  • You notice a smell of gas, burning oil, or coolant inside or around the car.
  • Fuel economy has dropped noticeably without an obvious reason.

If any of that sounds familiar, it's worth a call. Catching a problem early is almost always simpler — and less costly — than waiting for it to get worse.

Why Auto Repair Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Newport drivers know Minnesota weather is hard on a vehicle. Cold starts put extra strain on batteries and starters, and a car that was running fine in October can suddenly struggle once temperatures drop. Road salt used to keep streets clear through Washington County works its way into components over the winter and speeds up wear on exhaust systems, brake lines, and suspension parts. Potholes that show up every spring after a freeze-thaw cycle can knock alignment out and stress steering components. That combination is exactly why general repair work tends to cluster around the changing seasons here — and why it pays to get a noise or a warning light checked out before winter really sets in, not after you're stranded in it.
