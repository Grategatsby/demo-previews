Signs You Need New Brakes

Brakes usually warn you before they fail completely. Watch for:

  • A high-pitched squeal or squeak when braking, especially at low speed.
  • Grinding or metal-on-metal noise, which usually means the pads are worn through.
  • A soft, spongy, or low brake pedal that sinks farther than it used to.
  • Vibration or pulsing through the pedal or steering wheel when braking.
  • The car pulling to one side under braking.
  • A brake warning light on the dash, or it's simply been a while since your last inspection.

If you're hearing any of that, don't wait — brake pads that wear down to metal can damage rotors, turning a simple pad job into a more expensive repair.

Why Brake Repair Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Stopping distance matters everywhere, but it matters more on icy roads through Newport, Cottage Grove, and the rest of Washington County. Road salt used to melt ice and keep streets passable is hard on brake lines, calipers, and hardware over a full winter, speeding up corrosion that a driver in a milder climate might never deal with. Cold temperatures also make brake fluid and rubber components stiffer, which is part of why a squeal that started in November is worth checking before it turns into a grind by February. A brake inspection before winter really sets in is one of the simplest ways to head off a bad surprise on an icy morning.
