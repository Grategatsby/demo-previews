Signs of Transmission Problems

Transmission trouble rarely shows up out of nowhere. Common warning signs include:

  • Slipping gears — the engine revs up without the car accelerating the way it should.
  • Hesitation or a delay when shifting from park into drive or reverse.
  • Rough, hard, or jerky shifts between gears.
  • A burning smell, which often points to overheating transmission fluid.
  • Grinding, whining, or clunking noises when the car shifts.
  • Fluid (usually reddish and slightly sweet-smelling) leaking under the car.

Any one of these on its own is worth a look. Together, they're a sign the transmission needs attention before a slipping gear becomes a transmission that won't engage at all.

Is a Rebuilt Transmission as Good as a New One?

A properly rebuilt or quality replacement transmission, installed correctly, can perform just as well as a new one for a fraction of the cost — which is why replacement, not just "new only," is often the practical option we discuss with customers. We'll walk you through the realistic choices for your specific vehicle, including a rebuilt unit, a quality used replacement, or new, and give you our honest opinion on which makes sense for the car you're driving and how long you plan to keep it.

Why Transmission Care Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Cold Minnesota temperatures make transmission fluid thicker and slower to circulate, which is part of why a transmission that shifted fine in September can suddenly feel hesitant or rough on a January morning. Stop-and-go driving through Newport, St Paul Park, and the rest of Washington County during winter also puts extra strain on a transmission that's already working harder in the cold. Keeping up with fluid service before winter sets in is one of the simplest ways to avoid a transmission problem showing up at the worst possible time.
