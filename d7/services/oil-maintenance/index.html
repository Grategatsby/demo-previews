How Often Should You Change Your Oil?

How often you should change your oil depends on your specific vehicle, the oil type it uses, and how you drive it — your owner's manual is the best source for the manufacturer's recommended interval, and synthetic oil generally goes longer between changes than conventional oil. Short trips, a lot of stop-and-go driving through Newport and Cottage Grove, towing, or extreme cold and heat can all mean your vehicle needs oil changed more often than the mileage number alone suggests. If you're not sure what your vehicle needs, tell us the make, model, and how you typically drive it, and we'll tell you honestly what interval makes sense.

What's Included in a Routine Maintenance Visit

A routine maintenance visit at LaMotte & Son starts with the oil and filter change, then moves into the multi-point inspection — checking belts, hoses, fluid levels, and other wear items while the car is already in the bay. If we spot something worth mentioning, like a belt showing cracks or a fluid running low, we'll tell you plainly what we saw and let you decide what to do about it, rather than pressuring you into work the car doesn't need yet. That's the same straightforward approach we bring to every repair, just applied to the maintenance visits that keep bigger repairs from happening in the first place.

Why Routine Maintenance Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Minnesota winters are hard on a vehicle in ways that make routine maintenance more than a formality. Cold starts put extra strain on an engine running old, thickened oil, and a car that's overdue for a change can struggle more on a frozen morning than one that's current. Road salt and winter grime also make it easier to miss a slow leak or a worn hose during a quick glance under the hood, which is exactly what the multi-point inspection during an oil change is meant to catch. Getting maintenance done before winter really sets in, rather than waiting until something fails on a cold morning, is one of the simplest ways Newport drivers avoid a bigger problem later.
