What Our Collision Repair Covers

Collision damage rarely stops at the panel you can see, so our repair work covers the full picture:

  • Body panel repair and replacement — fenders, doors, hoods, and quarter panels straightened or replaced as needed.
  • Welding and structural repair — when impact damage goes past sheet metal into the frame or supporting structure.
  • Grille, headlight, and trim replacement — sourcing and installing parts that match your vehicle.
  • Color-matched paint and refinishing — including hard-to-match metallic and pearl finishes.
  • Bumper repair and replacement — the part of a collision that takes the first hit, more often than not.
  • Rust and corrosion repair — addressed as part of the repair when a collision exposes bare metal.

Whatever an accident did to your vehicle, we work through it panel by panel until it's back to normal.

Signs You Need Collision Repair

Some collision damage is obvious. Some isn't, and it's worth having it looked at anyway:

  • Visible dents, creases, or crumpled sheet metal after an accident or deer strike.
  • A bumper that's cracked, hanging, or torn loose.
  • Doors, a hood, or a trunk that don't line up or close flush the way they used to.
  • Paint that's scraped down to bare metal, which can rust quickly if it sits.
  • A headlight, grille, or piece of trim that's cracked or missing after an impact.
  • Any accident involving another vehicle, a deer, or a fixed object, even at low speed.

Even a collision that looks minor from the outside can hide damage underneath, so it's worth having it checked before you assume it's just cosmetic.

Why Collision Repair Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Winter driving in South St Paul brings more collisions, plain and simple — ice, snow, and deer crossing the road all add up to more accidents between November and March. It's also the season where fast repair matters most: any spot where paint is scraped away or a panel seam is opened up is an invitation for road salt and moisture to start rust underneath, and that damage spreads a lot faster in freeze-thaw conditions than most drivers expect. A dent or scraped panel that would just be cosmetic in July can turn into a rust problem by spring if it isn't addressed. That's why we treat every collision repair, even a small one, as worth doing right the first time.
