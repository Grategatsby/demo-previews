What Our Frame & Structural Repair Covers

Structural damage takes different forms depending on how a vehicle was hit, so our frame work covers the range:

  • Frame straightening — pulling and straightening a bent frame rail or unibody section back to proper alignment.
  • Structural welding — repairing or replacing sections of frame or unibody that are too damaged to straighten.
  • Suspension and mounting point repair — addressing the points where suspension and other components attach, when impact has moved them.
  • Alignment verification — checking that the repaired structure sits square before the vehicle goes back together.
  • Related body and paint work — panels and paint restored to match once the structural repair underneath is complete.

Structural repair is almost always paired with the body and paint work needed to finish the job, and we handle both under one roof.

Signs You Need Frame Straightening

Frame and structural damage isn't always obvious from the outside. Here's what tends to point to it:

  • The vehicle pulls to one side or feels unstable at speed after an accident.
  • Doors, the hood, or the trunk no longer line up or close flush.
  • Uneven tire wear that shows up soon after a collision.
  • Visible gaps or misalignment between body panels that weren't there before.
  • A moderate-to-hard impact — a collision, a hard pothole hit, or a deer strike — even if the surface damage looks minor.
  • A steering wheel that sits off-center or a car that no longer tracks straight.

If a vehicle was hit hard enough to raise any of these signs, it's worth having the frame checked rather than assuming the damage stopped at the panel.

What to Expect

We start by inspecting the vehicle's structure to see how far the damage actually extends — not just the panel that took the hit, but the frame or unibody sections connected to it. If a section can be pulled back into alignment, we straighten it; if it's damaged beyond that point, we weld in new structure. Once the frame or unibody is square again, we move into the body and paint work needed to finish the repair. Structural jobs take longer than a straightforward panel repair because the work has to be right before anything gets buttoned back up, and we'll walk you through the timeline once we've seen the damage.

Why Frame Repair Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

South St Paul's frost-heave roads and pothole season put more stress on a vehicle's frame than most drivers realize — a hard hit on a frozen pothole can jar a frame or suspension mounting point out of alignment even without a dramatic-looking dent to show for it. Add in winter collisions from ice and snow, and structural damage becomes more common exactly when it's hardest to spot from the outside. Left unaddressed, a misaligned frame also wears tires unevenly and stresses suspension components, so catching it early — even after what looks like a minor incident — saves more than just a straighter car.
