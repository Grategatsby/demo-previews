Body Work That Brings Your Vehicle Back

A parking lot dent, a scrape along a garage wall, or damage from a collision doesn't just look bad — it can expose bare metal to Minnesota's road salt and turn into rust if it sits too long. Our body work service covers the repairs that get your vehicle's panels, bumpers, and body lines back to where they should be, for drivers throughout South St Paul and the surrounding south metro.

What Our Body Work Covers

Body work spans a wide range of damage, from small and cosmetic to more involved:

  • Dents and dings — from shopping carts, hail, or a tight parking spot.
  • Scrapes and scratches — down to bare metal or through multiple paint layers.
  • Bumper damage — cracks, gouges, and impact damage front or rear.
  • Collision repair — panels and body components affected by an accident.
  • Rust repair — addressing corrosion before it spreads, especially where road salt has taken hold.
  • Panel alignment — doors, hoods, and fenders that no longer sit flush after an impact.

Bring the vehicle in and describe what happened — we'll take a look and explain what it'll take to fix it.

Signs Your Vehicle Needs Body Work

  • A dent or crease you can see or feel when you run a hand over the panel.
  • Scraped paint that's exposed bare metal, especially anywhere road salt can reach it.
  • A bumper that's cracked, sagging, or doesn't sit flush anymore.
  • Rust bubbling under the paint, particularly around wheel wells and rocker panels.
  • A door, hood, or trunk that doesn't close smoothly since an impact.

Small cosmetic damage rarely needs to be fixed the same day, but once bare metal is exposed, getting it addressed sooner keeps a scrape from becoming a rust repair.

What to Expect from a Body Work Visit

Bring your vehicle in and we'll look over the damage together, so you can point out exactly what's bothering you and ask questions as we go. We explain what caused the damage to look the way it does, what repairing it involves, and what the finished result will look like before any work begins. Appointments are recommended so we can plan the work properly, and we'll keep you posted on timing as the job moves along.

Body work generally involves a few stages: assessing how far the damage extends beneath the surface, repairing or replacing the affected panel, and blending the paint so the repair doesn't stand out from the rest of the vehicle. On a dent or crease, that often means reshaping the metal before anything else happens. On a scrape or rust spot, it usually means treating the affected area and any surrounding metal the damage has started to compromise. We walk you through which of those steps your vehicle needs before we start, so there are no surprises partway through.

Why Body Work Matters in South St Paul Winters

Minnesota winters are hard on a vehicle's body. Road crews salt South St Paul, West St Paul, and Inver Grove Heights streets heavily to keep them safe, and that salt is exactly what accelerates rust once paint is broken by a scrape, chip, or dent. A small scratch that would just be cosmetic in a warmer, drier climate can turn into a real corrosion problem here if it sits through a winter unaddressed. Getting body damage repaired promptly — especially anything down to bare metal — is one of the most effective ways south metro drivers protect a vehicle's long-term value.
