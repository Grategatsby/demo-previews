Not every car problem is something you can just drive over to us. Sometimes the car won't start at all, sometimes it's not safe to be on the road, and sometimes an accident or a mechanical failure has taken the decision out of your hands. That's what our towing service is for — we help arrange getting a vehicle that can't be safely driven to our shop at 103 5th Ave N, so drivers across South St Paul, West St Paul, Inver Grove Heights, and the surrounding south metro have a clear next step instead of guessing what to do with a car that's stuck.

What Our Towing Service Covers

Towing at Marie Avenue Service is about bridging the gap between a vehicle that's stuck and the repair it needs. That includes:

  • Vehicles that won't start — a dead battery, a failed starter, or an electrical problem that leaves the car unresponsive.
  • Vehicles that can't be safely driven — brake problems, cracked or damaged glass, body damage from an accident, or anything that makes driving the car risky.
  • Coordinating the tow around our business hours — we work with you to schedule the tow so your vehicle arrives when we're open and ready to look at it.
  • Getting stranded drivers off busy roads — if you're stuck somewhere along a road in or near South St Paul, we help get you and your vehicle out of a risky spot and into our shop.

The goal is simple: get your vehicle out of a bad spot and into our bay, where we can actually diagnose and fix what's wrong.

Signs You Need a Tow Instead of Driving In

Some problems are fine to nurse to our shop. Others aren't worth the risk. Consider a tow if:

  • The car won't start at all, no matter what you try.
  • A warning light is on alongside a loud or unfamiliar noise that makes driving feel unsafe.
  • You have a flat tire and no usable spare to get you here.
  • You notice fluid leaking heavily from underneath the vehicle.
  • You've been in an accident and the vehicle is no longer drivable.
  • The brakes don't feel safe — soft, grinding, or not responding the way they should.

If you're not sure whether your car is safe to drive, call us and describe what's going on. We'd rather help you make that call than have you take a chance on the road.

Why a Tow Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

A car that won't start on a subzero South St Paul morning is one of the most common calls we get once winter sets in — cold snaps are hard on batteries, starters, and anything electrical. Driving on bad brakes or a compromised vehicle is also a bigger risk when the roads are covered in snow and ice, so a tow during business hours can get a stranded vehicle off the road and into diagnosis faster than waiting it out on your own. Road salt and the potholes that show up through a Dakota County winter can also do enough damage that a vehicle isn't safe to drive even a short distance, which is exactly the kind of situation towing is built to solve.
