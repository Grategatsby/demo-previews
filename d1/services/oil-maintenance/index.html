What's Included in Our Oil Change & Maintenance Service

An oil change here is never just the oil — it's a chance to catch small issues early:

  • Fluid level checks — coolant, brake fluid, washer fluid, and power steering fluid where applicable.

The goal is simple: leave with more information about your car's condition than you came in with.

How Often Should You Get an Oil Change?

The right interval depends on your vehicle, the oil type, and how you drive — your owner's manual is the best starting point, and we'll factor in your specific vehicle when you bring it in. Signs it's time even if you're not sure of the exact mileage:

  • Your dashboard oil-change reminder light is on.
  • The oil looks dark and gritty on the dipstick rather than amber and clear.
  • You hear more engine noise than usual, especially right after starting.
  • It's simply been longer than the interval your owner's manual recommends.

When in doubt, ask — we'll check your oil condition and give you a straight answer about whether it's due.

Why Maintenance Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Cold Minnesota starts put extra strain on engine oil, especially oil that's already past its interval — thicker, degraded oil doesn't circulate and protect moving parts the way fresh oil does on a sub-zero morning. Road salt and grime on the White Bear Avenue corridor also work their way into belts and hoses over a winter, which is exactly what our multi-point check is looking for. Heading into winter with fresh oil and a car that's been looked over is one of the simplest ways to avoid a cold-morning breakdown.
