What Our Transmission Service Covers

Transmission work spans a range of jobs, and we look at the whole system before recommending a fix:

  • Transmission diagnosis — identifying why a transmission is slipping, shifting hard, or hesitating.
  • Transmission fluid changes — replacing fluid that's worn out, discolored, or overdue for service.
  • Leak diagnosis and repair — tracking down seals, gaskets, or lines that are letting fluid escape.
  • Shift quality checks — evaluating how the transmission shifts through the full gear range.
  • Warning light diagnosis — figuring out what's triggering a transmission or check-engine light related to shifting.
  • Clutch and linkage inspection — on vehicles where those components affect shifting and engagement.

If it affects how your car shifts or engages gears, it's worth having us take a look.

Signs of Transmission Problems

Transmission issues rarely show up out of nowhere — they usually give some warning first:

  • Slipping gears, or the engine revving without the car accelerating to match.
  • Rough, delayed, or hard shifting between gears.
  • A grinding, whining, or clunking noise when shifting.
  • Trouble engaging gears, especially reverse.
  • A burning smell, which can point to overheating transmission fluid.
  • Reddish fluid spots underneath the vehicle where it's parked.
  • A transmission or check-engine warning light on the dash.

Transmission problems tend to get more expensive the longer they're ignored, so catching one of these early is almost always the better outcome.

Why Transmission Care Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Cold Minnesota temperatures thicken transmission fluid, which can make shifting feel rougher or more hesitant during the first few minutes of a cold start, especially with fluid that's already past its service life. The stop-and-go driving that comes with snow and ice on the White Bear Avenue corridor and across St Paul's East Side also puts extra strain on a transmission that's already showing early warning signs. Staying ahead of a fluid service or catching a slip before winter sets in tends to mean a smaller repair now instead of a bigger one once the cold weather makes things worse.
