Signs Your Vehicle Needs Repair

Cars are good at telling you something's wrong before it becomes an emergency, if you know what to listen for:

  • A dashboard warning light that's on or flashing.
  • A new noise — clunking, squealing, ticking, or rattling.
  • Trouble starting, or a battery that seems to be dying faster than it used to.
  • The temperature gauge running hotter than normal, or visible steam or coolant under the car.
  • A rough idle, hesitation, or a loss of power when accelerating.
  • Fluid spots under the vehicle where it's parked overnight.

Catching any of these early is almost always cheaper and simpler than waiting until it strands you.

Why Auto Repair Matters More in a Minnesota Winter

Road salt on the White Bear Avenue corridor and across St Paul's East Side accelerates rust on exhaust components and undercarriage parts over a winter, and the freeze-thaw cycles every spring open up potholes that jolt suspension and steering components.
